# Pipeline Restoration - Validate Before Automation

## Purpose
Use this checklist before building deeper automation such as:
- tokenized intake flow
- upload portal
- OAuth / CRM connection flow
- automated reply sync
- deeper booking automation

The goal is simple:
**do not automate assumptions. Validate reality first.**

---

# What Must Be Proven First

## 1. Offer Validation
Before automation, prove the offer itself is real and wanted.

### Validate
- [ ] people respond to outreach
- [ ] discovery calls happen
- [ ] prospects clearly understand the problem
- [ ] prospects are willing to pay for help recovering dormant leads
- [ ] at least one pilot is agreed to

### Why it matters
If the offer itself is weak, more automation just makes a weak offer more efficient.

---

## 2. Data Access Validation
Before automation, prove clients can actually give you what the system needs.

### Validate
- [ ] client can provide CRM access OR exportable lead data
- [ ] data includes names, contact details, dates, notes, or lead history
- [ ] exported data is usable enough to work with
- [ ] you understand the minimum viable input format

### Why it matters
Do not build upload or auth flows until you know what data clients can realistically provide.

---

## 3. Workflow Validation
Before automation, prove the operator workflow works in the real world.

### Validate
- [ ] audit runs on real client data
- [ ] scoring feels directionally accurate
- [ ] drafted messages feel usable
- [ ] report is understandable to the client
- [ ] reply classification helps decision-making
- [ ] booking flow reflects what really happens next

### Why it matters
Manual-first reveals what actually works, where the process is clunky, and which steps deserve automation.

---

## 4. Conversion Validation
Before automation, prove the system can create measurable movement.

### Validate
- [ ] at least one real reply happens
- [ ] at least one warm reply happens
- [ ] at least one booking or clear booking opportunity happens
- [ ] recovered revenue is possible and trackable

### Why it matters
If the workflow has not yet proven it can create movement, automation is premature.

---

## 5. Friction Validation
Before automation, identify the actual bottleneck.

### Validate
- [ ] identify the most time-consuming step
- [ ] identify the most error-prone step
- [ ] identify the step clients struggle with most
- [ ] identify the step that most blocks revenue

### Why it matters
You should automate the highest-friction, highest-value step first — not the step that merely seems impressive.

---

## 6. Client Behavior Validation
Before automation, understand how real clients behave.

### Validate
- [ ] clients actually send required data
- [ ] clients move quickly enough for the workflow to work
- [ ] clients understand the report
- [ ] clients can approve or review messaging
- [ ] clients prefer either done-for-you or collaborative execution

### Why it matters
Automation should match real client behavior, not idealized behavior.

---

## 7. Proof Asset Validation
Before automation, prove the system creates usable proof.

### Validate
- [ ] screenshots can be captured
- [ ] metrics are meaningful enough to show
- [ ] case study can be written
- [ ] testimonial can be requested
- [ ] the pilot creates reusable authority assets

### Why it matters
One real proof point usually matters more than one extra automation layer.

---

# Automation Readiness Threshold
You are ready to build deeper automation when the following are true:

- [ ] one real pilot is complete
- [ ] one case study exists
- [ ] one testimonial exists
- [ ] one recovered-revenue result exists
- [ ] the biggest bottleneck is obvious
- [ ] the same friction appears repeatedly

If these are not true yet, stay focused on validation.

---

# What to Automate First After Validation

## 1. Highest-friction intake step
Examples:
- upload flow
- intake token
- CRM connection flow

## 2. Highest-frequency operator step
Examples:
- reply ingestion
- booking-link sending
- report generation
- objection follow-up drafting

## 3. Highest-value conversion step
Examples:
- booking automation
- warm lead routing
- reply-triggered scheduling

---

# Rules for Good Automation Decisions

## Do not automate:
- because it looks impressive
- because it would feel more “software-like”
- because you assume it will help
- before real usage proves the need

## Do automate:
- what repeatedly slows delivery
- what repeatedly creates confusion or errors
- what repeatedly blocks revenue
- what repeatedly appears across multiple pilots

---

# Short Version
Before deeper automation, prove:

1. the offer sells  
2. clients can provide usable data  
3. the workflow works on real data  
4. replies happen  
5. booking can happen  
6. revenue can be tracked  
7. the real bottleneck is obvious  

---

# Final Reminder
Do not automate imagination.
Automate reality.

The first pilot should tell you what deserves to be automated next.
