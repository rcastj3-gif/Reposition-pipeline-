# Pipeline Restoration - Pilot Target Scorecard

## Purpose
Use this scorecard before outreach so you can identify which businesses are actually strong pilot targets.

The goal is simple:
**do not waste time pitching businesses that cannot produce a fast, visible win.**

---

# How to Use This
Score each category from **0 to 4**.

- **0 = very weak**
- **1 = weak**
- **2 = workable**
- **3 = strong**
- **4 = ideal**

At the end, total the score.

---

# 1. Lead Volume
**Question:** Do they have enough leads in their CRM to make dormant lead recovery worthwhile?

### Score
- **0** = almost no leads
- **1** = very low volume, unclear database
- **2** = 100–200 leads
- **3** = 200–500 leads
- **4** = 500+ leads

### Notes
A business with no meaningful lead volume is not a strong target for this offer.

**Score:** ____

---

# 2. Lead Flow
**Question:** Are leads entering the business consistently?

### Score
- **0** = no clear inbound lead flow
- **1** = occasional leads only
- **2** = some inbound / inconsistent lead generation
- **3** = regular lead flow from one main source
- **4** = strong lead flow from multiple sources (ads, referrals, SEO, social)

### Notes
Dormant lead recovery works best when the business already generates leads and has leakage.

**Score:** ____

---

# 3. CRM Maturity
**Question:** Are they actually using a CRM well enough for this system to work?

### Score
- **0** = no CRM
- **1** = spreadsheet / poor tracking
- **2** = CRM exists but messy or incomplete
- **3** = active CRM usage
- **4** = active CRM with decent notes, statuses, or tags

### Notes
No CRM = no system to recover from.

**Score:** ____

---

# 4. Follow-Up Weakness
**Question:** Is poor follow-up clearly costing them money?

### Score
- **0** = follow-up already strong
- **1** = mild follow-up gaps
- **2** = visible inconsistency
- **3** = major leakage / slow follow-up / dropped leads
- **4** = follow-up is obviously broken and costing opportunities

### Notes
This is the pain point you want to solve.

**Score:** ____

---

# 5. Sales Process Strength
**Question:** If a lead re-engages and books, can they actually close?

### Score
- **0** = no real sales process
- **1** = weak close rate / unclear offer
- **2** = average sales ability
- **3** = solid close process
- **4** = strong close process and clear offer

### Notes
Do not choose a pilot where the real problem is bad closing, not bad follow-up.

**Score:** ____

---

# 6. Ticket Size / Revenue Potential
**Question:** Is the average client value high enough to create meaningful recovery fast?

### Score
- **0** = under $200
- **1** = $200–500
- **2** = $500–1,000
- **3** = $1,000–3,000
- **4** = $3,000+

### Notes
Higher ticket creates cleaner proof faster.

**Score:** ____

---

# 7. Speed to Win
**Question:** How quickly can this business convert reactivated leads into revenue?

### Score
- **0** = very long sales cycle
- **1** = slow sales cycle
- **2** = moderate timeline
- **3** = fast booking + closing path
- **4** = very fast path to appointment and revenue

### Notes
Your first pilot should create a visible win quickly.

**Score:** ____

---

# 8. Owner / Team Responsiveness
**Question:** Will they actually move fast enough to make the pilot work?

### Score
- **0** = flaky / unresponsive
- **1** = slow communicator
- **2** = average responsiveness
- **3** = responsive and cooperative
- **4** = highly responsive and action-oriented

### Notes
A great system with a slow client becomes a bad pilot.

**Score:** ____

# Score Interpretation

## 24–32 = Strong Pilot Target
Pitch now.
This is where you are most likely to get a clear win, usable proof, and a case study.

## 16–23 = Possible Target
Could work, but only pursue if:
- relationship is warm
- niche is strong
- owner is responsive
- there is enough upside

## 0–15 = Weak Pilot Target
Do not use as your first pilot.
You are likely forcing the wrong fit.

---

# Ideal First-Pilot Profile
Your best first pilot target looks like this:

- lots of leads
- weak follow-up
- strong offer
- decent close rate
- high enough ticket size
- fast path to revenue
- responsive owner

## Simple Summary
**Leads are there. Sales are decent. Follow-up is the leak.**

That is the sweet spot.

---

# Best Niches for First Pilot

## Strongest
- med spa
- dental
- home services
- legal
- coaching / consulting with inbound leads

## Weaker Choices
- e-commerce
- low-ticket offers
- no-CRM businesses
- businesses with no appointment model
- businesses with weak sales process but good follow-up

---

# Quick Qualification Prompt
Before outreach, ask yourself:

1. Do they have enough leads?
2. Is money leaking through follow-up gaps?
3. Can they actually close if we reactivate leads?
4. Will this produce a visible win in 30 days?
5. Will the owner move fast enough to make this work?

If the answer to most of those is no, move on.
