# Pipeline Restoration - Pilot Client Onboarding

## Purpose
This document is the ready-to-send onboarding asset for a real pilot client.

Use it after a client agrees to a pilot so you can collect everything needed to run the first audit cleanly.

---

# Welcome

Thanks for joining the **Pipeline Restoration pilot**.

The goal of this pilot is simple:
identify dormant opportunities already sitting in your CRM, reactivate the highest-value ones, and turn them into booked appointments and recovered revenue.

This process works best when we move fast, keep communication clear, and get the right access up front.

---

# What We Need From You

## 1. Business Information
Please send:
- Business name
- Main contact name
- Best email address
- Best phone number
- Website
- Industry / niche

---

## 2. HighLevel Access
Please provide:
- **HighLevel API key**
- **HighLevel location ID**

If you are not sure where to find these, we can walk through it together.

---

## 3. Offer Information
Please answer:
- What service / offer do you want us to reactivate?
- What is your average sale value?
- What is your typical booking conversion rate, if known?
- What is your typical close rate after booking, if known?

---

## 4. Lead Context
Please answer:
- Roughly how many contacts are in your CRM?
- Roughly how many leads do you believe are dormant?
- How long do leads usually sit before going cold?

---

## 5. Objections + Sales Context
Please answer:
- What are the most common objections you hear?
  - Price
  - Timing
  - Trust
  - Need to think about it
  - Other
- What usually helps people say yes?
- Do you offer payment plans or financing?
- If yes, how does that work?

---

## 6. Booking Information
Please send:
- Your booking link
- Your preferred call-to-action for warm leads

Examples:
- Book a consultation
- Schedule a call
- Reply for available times

---

## 7. Brand + Communication Guidance
Please answer:
- How should outreach feel?
  - Personal
  - Professional
  - Warm
  - Direct
  - Premium
  - Conversational
- Is there anything we should avoid saying?
- Are there any words, phrases, or claims you do not want used?

---

## 8. Pilot Permissions
Please confirm:
- Can results be used in an anonymized case study?
- Can screenshots be used if identifying details are removed?
- Can we request a testimonial if results are strong?

---

# What Happens Next

## Phase 1 - Audit
We will:
1. connect to your CRM
2. identify dormant leads
3. score the highest-potential opportunities
4. generate draft outreach
5. prepare your report

## Phase 2 - Review
You will:
1. review the report
2. confirm payment / approval
3. approve message direction

## Phase 3 - Execution
We will:
1. work the highest-priority leads first
2. classify replies
3. route warm leads toward booking
4. track outcomes and recovered value

---

# Pilot Success Criteria

## Minimum success
- audit completed successfully
- report delivered
- at least one warm reply identified

## Strong success
- one or more appointments booked
- recovered revenue tracked
- testimonial received

## Best-case success
- pilot clearly proves value
- client wants ongoing engagement
- case study becomes reusable proof asset

---

# Best Practices For Fastest Results
To make this pilot work as well as possible:
- send access quickly
- respond to review questions quickly
- approve top outreach quickly
- share booking links and objections clearly
- keep communication simple and direct

Speed helps momentum.
